About The New Democratic Party

The New Democratic Party is a social democratic federal political party in Canada founded in 1961.
  • NDP would enforce a mandatory vaccination policy. Those who refuse CoVID-19 vaccine could face discipline.
  • The NDP would also like to provide paid time off for workers to get vaccinated.
  • They have a billion-dollar plan to increase vaccination rates that target remote parts of Canada and vaccine-hesitant groups.
  • The NDP has made a promise to reduce emissions by at least 50 percent from 2005 levels by 2030.
  • They would end fossil fuel subsidies and stop providing support for pipelines.
  • Focus on expanding public transit with the goal of electrification.
  • Plans for $214.5 billion in new spending over 5 years
  • $166.3 billion in new revenue over five years
  • $314 billion expected cumulative budget deficit over five years
  • Plans to make home inspections mandatory when selling a home
  • 20% tax on homes purchased by foreign buyers
  • Plans for 500,000 "quality and affordable" housing units within 10 years
  • Open borrowing restrictions to allow people to buy a home with friends and roommates
  • $20 minimum wage
  • More than 1 million jobs will be created in first mandate
  • REquire large employers to spend at least one percent of payroll on training for their employees annually
  • Make CEOs who received federal subsidies intended for protecting workers' jobs pay the money back
  • One percent tax on households with fortunes topping $10 million
  • Income tax hike of two points to 25 percent for the highest bracket ($216,511+)
  • Increate the capital gains tax inclusion rate to 75 percent from 50 percent
  • Guarantee livable income for seniors and Canadians with disabilities
  • Remove control from big corporations to take profit out of long-term care
  • Until small businesses can fully reopen after Covid, they will continue providing wage and rent subsidies
  • Cap credit card merchant fees at one percent
  • Plans to cover the employer's portion of EI and CPP benefits for new and rehired staff by implementing a hiring bonus
  • Implement a price limit on mobile phone and Internet bills
  • Declare high-speed internet an essential service
  • Expand cell coverage and deliver internet to every Canadian community
  • Support fair trade in all parts of the country that broadens opportunity
  • Defend Canadian workers in trade negotiations by protecting supply management and standing up against unfair tariffs
  • National child-care program ($10-a-day)
  • Invest in children and ending funding discrimination
  • Close the education gap by providing Indigenous communities a safe place to learn and opportunity to succeed
  • Create jobs that expand opportunities for Indigenous peoples, and support local economic development by working with Indigenous entrepreneurs
